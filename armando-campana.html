Coaching Notes
Three-Year Progression
Improved his shot put every single year — 35'1" to 36'6" to 40'5". The discus curve is even steeper: 94'6" to 106'1" to 113'5". That's a 19-foot gain in the disc across three seasons, which is substantial development for a throws athlete. He peaked at the right time, setting career PRs deep into his senior season.
Senior Season Breakout
The jump from junior to senior year was his biggest: +3'10" in shot put and +7'4" in discus. He cleared 40 feet in the shot put for the first time during a dual meet versus Patriot and then backed it up with 40'2" two meets later. Hit 110'+ in discus multiple times down the stretch, finishing with a career-best 113'5" at League Championships.
Top 10 in River Valley
Finished 8th in the River Valley League in both shot put and discus as a senior — top 10 in both disciplines simultaneously. For a program that doesn't have a throws specialist coach on staff, that's a strong result. With dedicated throws coaching his senior year, his ceiling was likely higher than these marks show.
