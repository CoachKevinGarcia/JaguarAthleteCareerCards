Coaching Notes
Versatility
Soriano is the most versatile athlete in this cohort — she competed in 100m, 200m, 400m, discus, 4×100, 4×200, 4×400, and SMR in a single season. That breadth is rare and tells you she was willing to fill any gap the team needed. The downside: spreading across 5 events and multiple relay legs likely limited how specialized she could get in any one.
400m — Best Event
Her 1:23.63 at La Sierra (2nd place, RV 63rd) is her most meaningful competitive mark — she ran it in a dual meet at the start of league season and it held as her PR all year. The 400m suits her profile: she doesn't have elite short-speed but she has endurance, competitive instinct, and the body to hold pace. If she ever focused on just the 400m, sub-1:20 is plausible.
Discus — Late Surprise
Only three discus entries in 2026, but she improved from 45'6" to 56'10" across three meets — winning both dual meet competitions. The 56'10" (RV 24th) is a legitimate mark for someone who only threw late in the season. With a full season of throws training, she could develop into a real contributor in the circle.
