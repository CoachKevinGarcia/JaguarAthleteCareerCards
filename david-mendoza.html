Coaching Notes
Consistency of Growth
Improved in both events every single season. Shot put went from 31'8" to 37'9" over three years — a 6'1" gain. Discus went from 73'8" to 110'5" — a 36'9" gain. That kind of consistent trajectory across three seasons is rare and reflects both coachability and physical development.
Discus Development
His discus has outpaced his shot put development on a percentage basis. Breaking 100' this season is a significant threshold. The RV 12th ranking in discus suggests real competitiveness at the regional level. The outlier 65'1" at Patriot was a bad-series meet, not his ceiling — the 110'5" at Ramona and 104'7" at Norte Vista bookend it correctly.
Shot Put Plateau
He threw within a narrow 36'3"–37'9" band for most of the senior season, with his PR coming in early March. He didn't extend past 37'9" at Raincross or the RVL Championships. The PR held but he wasn't building on it — worth examining technically whether the shot mechanics were consistent with the discus growth.
Outlook
A throws athlete who improved every year through 12th grade, with no regression. The discus trajectory especially — if he competes at the collegiate club or JUCO level — suggests continued growth is achievable. College-level coaching, weight room access, and full-time throws focus would give him the best chance to extend his ceiling.
