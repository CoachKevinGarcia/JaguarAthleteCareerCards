Coaching Notes
Sub-60 400m
Going from a 29-second 200m on JV to a 59.71 400m on varsity in one year is a significant jump. He went sub-60 for the first time in a dual meet versus Patriot — not at a championship meet — which suggests he still had room to run. His 4×400 relay leg times at League Championships (3:55.9 team) reflect someone who can hold form at the end of a race.
400m as Primary Event
He ran the 100 and 200 but his best rankings are all in the 400. He's more of a one-lap runner who also competes in shorter sprints than a pure 100/200 guy. That sub-60 in just his first full varsity season with the 400 is a strong foundation. With a dedicated development plan in the off-season, mid-to-high 58s were a realistic ceiling.
JV to Varsity Leap
Ran only JV in 11th grade — 13.84c 100m, 29.34c 200m. Came back senior year as a fully varsity contributor anchoring relay legs and running sub-60. That development arc in a single off-season is one of the more dramatic improvements in the 2026 senior class.
